# Cellular Automata Model for Enzymatic Degradation of Semicrystalline Polymers

This repository contains the Python and MATLAB codes developed for the mathematical and computational modelling of enzymatic degradation of semicrystalline biodegradable polymers.

The framework combines:

- Cellular Automata (CA) modelling of polymer microstructure evolution
- Reaction–diffusion modelling of enzyme and oligomer transport
- Chain-length distribution tracking
- Molecular weight evolution
- Product formation and transport
- Erosion and porosity development
- Parameter estimation and calibration
- Bayesian uncertainty quantification
- Global sensitivity analysis (Sobol indices)
- Grid convergence and geometry-transferability studies

## Repository Structure

- **PCL6300** – Simulations and analyses for PCL6300.
- **PCL6500** – Simulations and analyses for PCL6500.
- **PCL6800** – Simulations and analyses for PCL6800.
- **PCL-Lipase** – Lipase-driven degradation studies.
- **Global sensitivity analysis (Sobol)** – Variance-based sensitivity analysis.
- **Uncertainty Analysis** – Bayesian uncertainty quantification and prediction intervals.
- **CA grid convergence and sensitivity analysis** – Grid refinement and convergence studies.
- **Different Geometry Study** – Investigation of geometry and thickness effects on degradation behaviour.

## Research Focus

The models are designed to investigate how polymer microstructure, enzyme transport, molecular weight reduction, oligomer formation, and erosion interact during enzymatic degradation. Applications include bioresorbable medical implants, tissue engineering scaffolds, and controlled drug delivery systems.
